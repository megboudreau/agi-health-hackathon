## Clinical Flags

### Severe
- **QT-prolongation risk – Domperidone + Quetiapine:** Both prolong the QT interval; domperidone carries a Health Canada warning for serious ventricular arrhythmia/sudden death, especially with other QT-prolonging agents and in older patients. Obtain baseline ECG and electrolytes; reconsider need for domperidone.
- **Hyperkalemia – Irbesartan (ARB) + Spironolactone:** RAAS + potassium-sparing diuretic combination, compounded by likely renal impairment (see below). Risk of dangerous hyperkalemia; requires close potassium and creatinine monitoring.

### Moderate
- **Probable renal impairment / renally-cleared drugs:** Allopurinol dosed only every 2 days strongly suggests reduced kidney function. **Pregabalin, famotidine, spironolactone, and chlorthalidone** all require renal-function review and possible dose adjustment; **spironolactone + ARB** is hazardous if eGFR is low.
- **Cumulative hypotension / fall risk:** Amlodipine + irbesartan + chlorthalidone + carvedilol (75 mg/day) + spironolactone + **prazosin TID** (alpha-blocker → first-dose and orthostatic hypotension). High orthostatic/fall burden, particularly with concurrent insulin and hypoglycemia risk.
- **Acid-suppression duplication:** Esomeprazole (PPI BID) + **famotidine (H2RA BID)** + **sucralfate** — triple overlapping acid/mucosal therapy. Reduces pill burden opportunity and famotidine is renally cleared.
- **Iron absorption interactions:** Ferrous sulfate absorption markedly reduced by esomeprazole, famotidine, and sucralfate; also binds **cholestyramine**. If iron deficiency anemia is being treated, current regimen may be ineffective.
- **Cholestyramine binding:** Sequesters many co-administered drugs (atorvastatin, irbesartan, thyroid agents, etc.). Must be separated by ≥4 h from other medications.

### Mild
- **Pregabalin duplication/titration ambiguity:** Both 25 mg and 50 mg capsules active — clarify whether cross-titration or unintended duplication.
- **Serotonergic/CNS additive sedation:** Duloxetine + pregabalin + quetiapine → additive sedation/dizziness; counsel and monitor.
- **Duloxetine + chlorthalidone/SSRIs-SNRIs:** Mild hyponatremia (SIADH) risk in older patient on thiazide — periodic sodium check.
- **Sucralfate + ophthalmic timing:** Sucralfate (needs acid) given with PPI/H2RA reduces its own efficacy.

1. **Order STAT/urgent labs: serum potassium, creatinine/eGFR, and sodium.** The irbesartan + spironolactone combination plus apparent renal impairment poses serious hyperkalemia risk — adjust or hold spironolactone if eGFR low or K⁺ elevated.
