## Medication List

- **Metformin** 1000 mg orally twice daily
- **Lisinopril** (Zestril) 10 mg orally once daily
- **Losartan** (Cozaar) 50 mg orally once daily
- **Amlodipine** (Norvasc) 5 mg orally once daily
- **Furosemide** (Lasix) 40 mg orally once daily
- **Atorvastatin** (Lipitor) 40 mg orally once daily at bedtime
- **Allopurinol** 300 mg orally once daily
- **Ferrous sulfate** 325 mg orally three times daily with food
- **Calcitriol** 0.25 mcg orally once daily
- **Sevelamer carbonate** (Renvela) 800 mg orally three times daily with meals
- **Ibuprofen** 400 mg orally as needed (PRN)
- **Pantoprazole** (Tecta) 40 mg orally once daily
- **Sertraline** (Zoloft) 50 mg orally once daily

## Clinical Flags

### Severe
- **Metformin + CKD stage 3b (eGFR ~35):** Metformin is contraindicated — risk of life-threatening lactic acidosis. Requires immediate discontinuation.
- **Dual RAAS blockade (Lisinopril + Losartan):** Combining an ACE inhibitor and an ARB is contraindicated — increases risk of acute kidney injury, hyperkalaemia, and hypotension with no proven benefit over monotherapy.
- **Ibuprofen + CKD:** NSAIDs are nephrotoxic and contraindicated in CKD. Regular use accelerates kidney function decline and blunts the antihypertensive and diuretic effect.

### Moderate
- **Allopurinol 300 mg + CKD stage 3b:** Allopurinol and its metabolite oxypurinol accumulate in renal impairment — risk of allopurinol hypersensitivity syndrome. Dose should be reduced to 100–150 mg/day.
- **Lisinopril + Furosemide:** Risk of first-dose hypotension and prerenal azotaemia, particularly if the patient is volume-depleted. Monitor BP and renal function after initiation or dose changes.
- **Ferrous sulfate + Calcitriol:** Iron reduces calcitriol absorption when taken simultaneously. Separate administration by at least 2 hours.

### Mild
- **Pantoprazole — possible prescription cascade:** Likely prescribed for GI side effects of ferrous sulfate. Consider switching to ferric gluconate before committing to long-term PPI therapy.
- **Sertraline in older adult:** Monitor for hyponatraemia (SIADH). Check baseline serum sodium before continuing.

## Inferred Diagnoses

- **Chronic Kidney Disease, stage 3b** — eGFR ~35 inferred from use of sevelamer, calcitriol, and allopurinol dose in context
- **Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus** — metformin
- **Hypertension** — lisinopril, losartan, amlodipine, furosemide
- **Gout** — allopurinol
- **Anemia of CKD / Iron deficiency anemia** — ferrous sulfate
- **Secondary hyperparathyroidism / CKD-mineral bone disorder** — calcitriol, sevelamer carbonate
- **Hyperphosphatemia** — sevelamer carbonate
- **Depression or anxiety disorder** — sertraline
- **GERD or PPI-dependent dyspepsia (possibly iatrogenic)** — pantoprazole; likely secondary to iron supplementation
- **Dyslipidaemia / Cardiovascular risk reduction** — atorvastatin

## Recommended Actions

1. **Discontinue metformin immediately** — switch to linagliptin 5 mg once daily (no renal dose adjustment required at this eGFR) or consult endocrinology for insulin initiation.
2. **Discontinue ibuprofen** — substitute acetaminophen 500–1000 mg PRN for pain management. Counsel patient to avoid all OTC NSAIDs.
3. **Discontinue losartan** — continue lisinopril as monotherapy (ACE inhibitor preferred in CKD with likely proteinuria). Monitor potassium and creatinine at 1–2 weeks.
4. **Reduce allopurinol to 100 mg/day** — recheck uric acid in 6–8 weeks and titrate cautiously if target not met.
5. **Separate ferrous sulfate and calcitriol** — administer calcitriol at bedtime and iron with meals at least 2 hours prior.
6. **Reassess pantoprazole** — trial ferric gluconate (better GI tolerability) in place of ferrous sulfate; discontinue PPI if GI symptoms resolve.
7. **Check serum sodium** before next sertraline refill; repeat at 4–6 weeks.
8. **Order renal function panel** (creatinine, eGFR, potassium, phosphate, PTH) to confirm CKD staging and guide further dose adjustments.
